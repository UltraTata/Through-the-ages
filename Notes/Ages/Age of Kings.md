Age of Kings is the second [[Age|age]] of the game. It starts in 2334 BC with the coronation of Sargon of Akkad and ends in 1213 BC with the death of Ramses II.

==Base deck==
**Commander** x5: [[Profit]] iron
**Merchant** x5: [[Profit]] gold
**Queen** x5: [[Profit]] jade
**Priest** x5: [[Profit]] marble
**Raider** x5: [[Attack]] any nation. If successful, steal half its gold.
**Envoy** x3: *Reactive*. When attacked, Spend 1 jade per iron the enemy spent on the attack to cancel it (the enemy won't lose the iron but you will use the jade)
**Scribe** x5: [[Research]] 1 [[Technology|technology]] or adopt heritage spending marble
**Just king** x3: [[Pass law]] or adopt heritage spending jade.
**Builder** x5: [[Build]] a building or adopt heritage spending gold.
**Builder king** x1: Build as many buildings as you like or adopt heritage spending gold.
**Law-giver** x1: Pass as many laws as you like or adopt heritage spending jade.
**Farmer** x5: Build as many farms or wheat fields as you like
**Peaceful king** x5: [[Grow population]]
**Expansionist king** x5: [[Migrate]] 1 population belonging to your nation per gold spent and [[Explore|explore]] 1 territory per iron spent. In that order.
**Conqueror** x5: Attack any nation. If successful conquer 1 territory
**Great conqueror** x1: Attack any nation. If successful conquer 1 territory. Repeat as many times as wanted. Only one time per nation.
**Guerrilla commander** x1: *Reactive*. Use after being defeated. Nullifies the effect of the attack. If coins were spent, they are lost.
**Militia commander** x1: *Reactive*. Use when attacked. x2 defense.
**Strategist** x1: *Reactive*. Use when attacking. x2 attack.
**Priest-king** x1: Assimilate 1 population under your control per marble spent.
**Spy** x1: *Reactive*. Use at any time. Learn the coin stock and reactions of one player.
**Vengeful King** x3: *Reactive*. Use after being attacked. Launch a [[Counterattack|counterattack]].

==Technological tree==
**Chronicles**: Costs 2 marble. Add 5 Chroniclers to your reshuffling deck (Gain 1 jade every 2 marble spent)
**Long-distance trade**: Costs 2 marble. Enables caravans and add 3 explorer merchants (profit gold and explore 1 territory per gold spent)
**Domestication of the Horse**: Costs 2 marble. x2 gold.
**War Horses**: Requires D. of the Horse. Costs 2 marble. x2 attack.
**Wheel**: Requires D. of the Horse. Costs 2 marble. +1 gold profit per territory. Enables road.
**War Chariots**: Requires War Horses and Wheel. Costs 3 marble. x2 attack x2 defense.
**Siege engineering**: Costs 2 marble. x2 attack.
**Military architecture**: Costs 2 marble. x2 defense. +1 defense per palisade. +1 defense per population in walls.
**Terracing**: Costs 2 marble. Enables terrace.
**Calendar**: Costs 2 marble. x2 marble.
**Espionage**: Costs 3 marble. Add 2 spies.
**Counter-espionage**: Requires espionage. Costs 3 marble. Add 3 sleeper agents (*Reactive.* Use upon being spied on. Deny the espionage, learn the coins and reactions of the nation who spied on you. Rob it of half its jade.)
**Official seals**: Costs 2 marble. x2 jade.
**Diplomacy**: Requires official seals. Costs 3 marble. Enables embassy. Add 3 interventionist kings (*Reactive.* Use after another player is attacked. Launch a counterattack. If successful, undo the effect of the war it is intervening in).
**Treaties**: Requires official seals. Costs 2 marble. Official treaties may be signed with other players, agreeing on an amount of jade each side has to have as a guarantee. If the treaty is broken, the other signatories receive said jade. A player can go on the negatives if the promised jade isn't in its savings. Only one of the signatories needs to have this technology.

==General building pool==
**Farm**: Costs 4 gold. +1 [[Grow population|population growth]]
**Road**: Requires Wheel. Costs 4 gold. +1 gold profit.
**Market**: Costs 8 gold. x2 gold profit
**Barracks**: Costs 8 gold. x2 iron profit
**Palace**: Costs 16 gold. x3 jade profit
**Temple**: Costs 8 gold. x2 marble profit.
**Palisade**: Costs 4 gold. +1 defense bonus
**Walls**: Costs 8 gold. +1 defense bonus per population.
**Caravans**: Requires long-distance trade. Costs 16 gold. x3 gold profit x3 marble profit
**Black-smith**: Costs 8 gold. For every pair of copper and tin, +1 attack and +1 defense
**Embassy**: Requires diplomacy. Costs 8 gold. Add 1 ambassador (*Reactive.* Use at the beginning of the turn of another player who has less ambassadors in their deck than you. See the leaders drawn by the target player throughout the whole turn) per population.

==Extractive building pool==
**Copper mine**: Costs 4 gold. Built on copper hills. Provides copper.
**Tin mine**: Costs 4 gold. Built on tin deposits. Provides tin.
**Canal**: Costs 8 gold. Built on rivers. +1 population growth to all territories with a farm or wheat field.
**Terrace**: Requires terracing. Costs 4 gold. Built on hills. +1 population growth per population.

==Law==

**The question of authority**
Choice costs 2 jade
A) *Divine kingship*: x2 jade profit
B) *Kings as servants of God*: x2 marble profit

**The question of law**
Choice costs 2 jade
A) *Royal law*: x2 jade profit.
B) *Divine law*: x3 jade profit in temples.
C) *Common law*: x2 marble profit.

**The question of diplomacy**
Choice costs 2 jade
A) *Isolationism*: x2 defense.
B) *Cultivate diplomatic bonds*: Envoys twice as effective.
C) *Expansionism*: x2 attack.

**The question of the army**
Choice costs 2 jade
A) *Levies*: x2 defense.
B) *Standing army*: x2 iron.
C) *Army maintained through raiding*: x4 attack while raiding. x2 earnings from raiding.
D) *Tributary armies*: May use envoys while attacking to earn +1 attack per jade spent. 

**The question of robbery**
Choice costs 2 jade
A) *Death for thieves*: x2 jade profit.
B) *Hand mutilation for thieves*: x2 marble profit.
C) *Fine for thieves*: x2 gold profit.
D) *Slavery for thieves*: x2 iron profit.

**The question of widows**
Choice costs 2 jade
A) *Widows taken care of by their brothers-in-law*: x2 gold.
B) *Widows taken care of by the community*: +1 marble profit per owned territory.
C) *Widows taken care of by the palace*: x2 marble profit in palaces.
D) *Widows taken care of by the temple*: x2 marble profit in temples.

==Heritage==

**King of the Totality**: Earned through achievements. Start every age with 3 iron.
**Code of Hammurabi**: Earned through achievements. Add 1 Law-giver to your deck.
**Ancient Great Power**: Earned through achievements. x2 attack when supporting an ally or in wars of intervention.

==Achievements==

**King of the Totality**
Conditions: Own 9 territories and defeat each player in an offensive war at least once.
Reward: Adopt "King of the Totality" heritage.

**Code of Hammurabi**
Conditions: Pass 6 laws.
Reward: Add Hammurabi to your deck and adopt "Code of Hammurabi" heritage.
Hammurabi: *Unique.* Pass as many laws as desired, half the price.

**Club of Great Powers**
Conditions: Have 1 tin and 1 copper per player.
Reward: Adopt "Ancient Great Power" heritage.

**Law of Moses** 
Conditions: Own 6 temples.
Rewards: Start the [[Dark Age]] with one law passed and add Moses to your Dark Age deck.
Moses: *Unique.* Explore a territory of your choice and migrate every population belonging to your nation outside your control to said territory.

**Survive the Late Bronze Age Collapse**
Conditions: Be the last to fall to the Sea Peoples.
Rewards: Start the Dark Age with the Diplomacy technology researched and 1 iron per turn of collapse that was endured.

==Events==

**The Late Bronze Age Collapse**
After King of the Totality, Code of Hammurabi, Club of Great Powers, and Law of Moses are taken; the Sea Peoples will flood the known world.

The strength of the Sea Peoples will start at 1. It will double each round.

Each player will be attacked by the Sea Peoples with their strength as attack points ==after== they draw their leader. If the Sea Peoples win, they will take all claimed territories and one owned territory at the defeated's choice from them.

The Sea Peoples may be aided or intervened against by other players like in a war between players.

During the Late Bronze Age Collapse, it is not allowed to declare [[Bankruptcy]]. When a player has 0 population, they are discarded for the rest of the age (which won't last for long anyway).

If all the remaining survivors are wiped out in the same round, all of them will earn the "Survive the Late Bronze Age Collapse" achievement.
